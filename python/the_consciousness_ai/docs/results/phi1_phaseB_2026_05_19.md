## Substantive scientific reading

The hypothesis Phase B was designed to test: "if AKOrN's pairwise phase coherence directly modulates how module content tensors blend, then phi-on-broadcast becomes structurally downstream of sync_R through TWO mechanisms (bid-weighting AND content-weighting), and Phi-1 should emerge."

This hypothesis is empirically REFUTED. Content-level binding via coherence-modulated cross-attention does not produce r > 0.15 on either pathway. The mechanism works at the attention-weight level (synthetic gate confirms it), but the downstream phi quantity does not track sync_R.

Three possible deeper explanations:

1. **AKOrN's phases are abstract, not content-bearing.** The phase vectors live in a separate space from content. When BindingAttention uses phase coherence to gate content blending, the resulting bound content carries the coherence structure but not the binding-relevant information that phi could detect.

2. **The chain from binding to phi is too indirect.** Each transformation in the pipeline (binding -> coherence -> BindingAttention -> Phase A fusion -> broadcast -> gate/RIIU -> phi) loses some signal.

3. **AKOrN itself may not be the right binding mechanism.** GASPnet (2024-2025) uses Kuramoto dynamics on COMPLEX-VALUED CONTENT vectors. The phase IS the phase of the content. This structurally couples binding and integration at the substrate level. AKOrN's separation of phases (abstract) from content (concrete) may be the structural issue that no amount of cross-attention can bridge.
